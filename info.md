### üéØ Executive Summary

The **National Democratic Alliance (NDA)** swept the Bihar Assembly Elections 2025 with a historic landslide victory, securing **202 out of 243 seats** (83%) ‚Äî their best performance since 2010. This represents a three-fourths majority and a crushing defeat for the Rashtriya Janata Dal (RJD)-led Mahagathbandhan, which won only 35 seats.

**Key Highlights:**
- **BJP** emerged as the single largest party for the first time in Bihar with **89 seats** (88.1% strike rate)
- **JD(U)** staged a massive comeback with **85 seats**, up from just 43 in 2020
- **RJD** collapsed to **25 seats** from 75 in 2020 ‚Äî their worst performance under Tejashwi Yadav
- Record **67.13% voter turnout** ‚Äî highest in Bihar's electoral history
- **Female turnout at 71.78%** vs male 62.98% ‚Äî a decisive 9% gender gap that favored NDA
- **Zero re-polls** for the first time in Bihar history

### üîë Key Factors Behind NDA Victory

**1. Women Voter Mobilization**
- Historic 71.78% female turnout (vs 62.98% male) created a decisive 9-point gender gap
- **Mukhyamantri Mahila Rojgar Yojana** (Rs 10,000 scheme launched in Sept 2025) resonated strongly
- Women credited welfare schemes for their loyalty

**2. EBC Consolidation**
- EBCs constitute 36% of Bihar's population (from 2023 caste survey)
- Nitish Kumar (Kurmi community) successfully consolidated EBC support
- Contrasted sharply with RJD's narrow Yadav-Muslim base

**3. Nitish Kumar Factor**
- Despite anti-incumbency and flip-flop criticism, Nitish's governance record held
- JD(U)'s remarkable turnaround: 43 seats (2020) ‚Üí 85 seats (2025)
- Infrastructure development and welfare schemes paid dividends

**4. BJP's Expansion**
- First time BJP emerged as single largest party in Bihar assembly
- Consolidated upper caste + Non-Yadav OBC + youth votes
- Strong organizational machinery and PM Modi's charisma

**5. Chirag Paswan's Strategic Win**
- LJP(RV) won 19/28 seats (65.5% strike rate)
- Consolidated Dalit/Paswan votes effectively
- Validated NDA alliance strategy

**6. Unemployment Narrative Failed**
- Despite 9.6% urban unemployment (5th highest in India), jobs issue didn't translate to votes
- Voters cited "fatigue" with unfulfilled promises
- Other factors (caste, welfare, identity) dominated inside polling booths

**7. Youth Vote Fragmentation**
- MGB expected youth consolidation around unemployment issue
- Instead, youth votes fragmented across NDA, MGB, Jan Suraaj, and AIMIM
- Urban/semi-urban youth leaned toward NDA in Patna, Muzaffarpur, Bhagalpur

***

### üìâ Why Mahagathbandhan Failed

**1. RJD's Narrow Base**
- Despite highest vote share (23%), couldn't convert to seats
- Over-dependent on Yadav-Muslim consolidation (which didn't materialize fully)
- Failed to expand beyond traditional 23% ceiling

**2. Congress Collapse**
- From 19 seats (2020) to just 6 seats (2025)
- Vote share dropped from 9.48% to 8.71%
- Weak grassroots organization in Bihar

**3. AIMIM Factor**
- Owaisi's AIMIM won 5 seats, mostly in Muslim-dominated constituencies
- Split Muslim votes that would have gone to MGB
- NDA also made inroads in Muslim-heavy seats

**4. Anti-Incumbency Didn't Work**
- Despite 20 years of Nitish Kumar governance, voters gave him another mandate
- Welfare schemes and development outweighed incumbency fatigue

**5. Prashant Kishor's Jan Suraaj**
- Divided opposition votes in 35 constituencies where margin < winning margin
- Though won zero seats, impacted MGB negatively
